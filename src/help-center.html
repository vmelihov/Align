Am I too old for college?
Age is only a number, and we believe everyone deserves their shot at having the best education. Maybe youʼve tried one area of study or started working straight out of high school and want to make a shift. Take that chance, youʼre never too old. Most college students, arenʼt straight out of high school.
How to choose the right college for me?
At the beginning of the application, our algorithm and graph indicate your lifetime revenue based on the education category you choose. You are free to select your occupation interest, we then align you with the best school that fits your criteria. Weʼve got career and college advisors that are standing by to get you set up.
Online College vs Physical Location
Weʼve got a great selection of both online school programs and programs in which youʼll need to physically attend a class. There are benefits to both. An online class gives you the benefit of taking the class from anywhere, as long as you have a computer and internet connection. While it does give you the flexibility; physical attendance gives you the benefit of interacting with your peers in person.
How do I get my credits transferred from a previous college?
The opportunity to transfer credits will depend on the school's accreditation. It also will depend on the school and program youʼll be applying for. Be sure to acquire additional information from the school you will be in contact with.
Should I visit colleges before choosing?
We highly recommend scheduling a tour with the prospective school youʼll be in contact with. Be sure to prepare a fresh list of questions to ask, while youʼll be touring the school.
What is meant by a school or college is accredited?
Accreditation is the process by which a schoolʼs curriculum is analyzed and approved by the accreditation body. The goal of accreditation is to ensure that education provided by institutions of higher education meets acceptable levels of quality. There is an online database of accredited colleges in the United States. Go and visit http://ope.ed.gov/accreditation/
What is the estimated cost of college?
The cost of college really depends on the program you choose and the school you decide to attend. There are a few factors to consider when thinking about school costs: tuition, housing, commute, books, extracurricular activities, etc. Please inquire with the school advisor you will be speaking with.
What are some examples of whatʼs involved in the process for college admission?
Once you are connected with a school advisor, there’s a few important tasks to consider to proceed. Often times there’s an admission test, an overview of previous GPA, and SAT scores. Expect to also have an in-person interview with the schoolʼs admission team, that may also request a written essay to be submitted to the school. It helps admissions officers understand who you are, what makes you unique and why this school and program are important to you.
What are the application deadlines and enrollment dates for colleges?
College applications and enrollment dates vary for every college. For some, it is open all year round while for others it depends on the class size, duration of courses/programs offered, state licensing requirements, etc. Itʼs best to contact the college you are considering as soon as you can and speak with their admissions department.
